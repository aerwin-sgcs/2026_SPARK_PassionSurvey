How to use the Spark app

A short guide to finding what you're curious about. Takes about 30 minutes the first time — and you can come back to it whenever you want.

The app shows you 55 areas of interest. You'll rate each one, see your top picks, and then turn one into something you can actually do this week. There are no right answers. Move fast — your first instinct is usually right.
★
The rating buttons
Used through every area

Each area has four buttons. Pick one:

✗ No thanks ~ Maybe ★ Interested ★★ Most interested

Rules:

  • You can only pick 3 as ★ and 3 as ★★.
  • Once you fill all 3, the buttons lock until you free a slot.
  • Tap the same button again to un-pick it.
  • No limit on ✗ or ~.
TipThe bar at the top of the app shows how many slots you've used. Watch it.
1
The survey
About 12 minutes
  1. Rate every one of the 55 areas. Don't skip — even a ✗ is useful.
  2. Trust your gut. Stuck? It's probably a ~.
  3. The progress bar shows how far through you are.
TipYou can change your mind any time before you click "See my passion profile".
Two helpers built into the survey:
  • The ? button next to each area's name tells you what that area actually is — what people who do this spend their time on. It also tells you which two Habits of Mind this work grows. Tap to read, tap again to close.
  • "Not sure where to start? Tap an interest →" at the very top opens a list of things you might already love — drawing, video games, animals, music, helping people, and many more. Tap one and the matching areas of the survey light up in orange. Tap a name to jump straight there. Pick a new interest any time, or tap Clear.

Before you see your results
A quick reflection (optional)

When you tap "See my passion profile", the app pauses on a single screen first. It asks three things before showing your sparks:

  • Which areas were easy to rate?
  • Which were hard?
  • What surprised you?

One textarea — write a sentence covering all three, or skip it entirely. Whatever you write is saved and travels into your copied results and PDF, under a "Before I knew my profile…" section.

The point is to notice yourself as a learner before the result lands. "Whatever you found — or didn't find yet — is information."
✦
Your results screen
After the reflection

Three zones — how does each top spark feel right now? Under each ★★ you'll see three pills:

  • Comfort — "I could do this with my eyes closed."
  • Learning — "Stretchy — possible if I try."
  • Panic — "Too much right now."

Tap whichever fits. Tap again to clear.

As soon as you tag a spark as Learning, a small gold callout appears: "Learning zone = where your brain grows the fastest. Consider starting your Phase 4 action here."
PanicIf you tag a spark as Panic, a small coral message appears under that spark: "Perhaps return to the first page and change your 3 star choice to something else." Panic is a sign that the ★★ might be too far out of reach right now — better to swap it for something you can actually start on.

Also explore: each ★★ also shows two related areas plus one "Another possibility:" suggestion — hints for what to look at next.

2
Ignite
5 minutes · tap Phase 4 button

This is the most important 5 minutes. Don't skip.

  1. Name your spark. Write one sentence: "I think I'm curious about ____ because ____." Be specific. Not "science" — say what about science.
  2. Choose one tiny action. Watch / Ask / Make / Read. Just one. Fifteen minutes max. You'll do it in the next 48 hours. Each action card shows a "Builds the habit of: …" line — tap the habit name to read a short pop-up explaining it.
  3. Say it out loud. Even just to yourself, or to someone you trust. Hearing yourself say it makes you far more likely to follow through.
  4. Come back later. Once you've done your action, open Phase 4 again. Step 4 has a purple check-in panel where you write:
    • What did you do to learn more about your passion area? (the action you took, which may not be the one you had planned)
    • What happened to your interest? (still there / got bigger / changed completely)
    • What did you find out about your strengths, the parts you struggled with, and how you like to learn?

Then tap "Generate my commitment card". Your spark sentence, action, Habit of Mind, and check-in (if you wrote one) all appear together — copy it or save as a PDF.

A feeling that doesn't become an action fades within days. This step is where you turn the feeling into something you'll actually do.
3
What's next?
Self-paced · tap Phase 5 button

Tap "Phase 5 — What's next?" to see places to go further.

  • Eight category buttons, one per area of interest.
  • Categories where you starred sparks are tinted in their colour with a small badge like "★★ 1 · ★ 2" — so you know at a glance which ones matter to you.
  • You can still tap any category, even ones you didn't star.

Inside each category:

  • Competitions you can enter
  • Free resources to explore
  • University-level resources for when you're ready to go deeper
Plus — questions you could ask an expert. Near the top of Phase 5 there's a small gold button: "Scroll down to see questions you could ask an expert ↓". Tap it to jump to a section at the bottom with 8 questions per category — to ask an adult who actually works in that area. Screenshot a category's questions before you talk to someone whose job sounds like your spark.
4
Use your spark in class
Self-paced · tap Phase 6 button

Tap "Phase 6 — Use your spark →". This is for when you're sitting in another lesson — English, Maths, History — and you want to make it more interesting.

How it works:

  1. Pick a spark — your sparks are at the top, colour-coded by category. Tap the spark name (shown with a small (?)) to see what it actually means.
  2. Pick the subject you're in — English, Maths, Science, etc.
  3. You'll see an amber line first: "Notice if this feels stretchy. Stretchy = your brain growing." Then two columns:
    • Left — five "moves" your spark teaches you to make
    • Right — four "hooks" where each subject lets you plug your spark in
  4. Pick one from each side. That's your hint for the lesson.

Want more options? Tap "+ Browse more sparks" to see every other area you haven't ruled out, grouped by category.

Your spark isn't just a topic — it's a way of thinking that travels into every subject.
↔
Jumping between phases
All three overlays

At the top of Phase 4, 5, and 6 there are small jump pills — gold for Phase 4, teal for Phase 5, purple for Phase 6. Tap one to switch phases directly, without going back through the survey.

↻
Will my answers be saved?
Auto-save

The app remembers your answers automatically on the iPad you used — your ratings, spark sentence, action choice, reflection, zone picks, and check-in. Close the tab and come back later — your work will be there. You'll see a small message at the bottom: "Restored your previous answers."

ImportantIt only works on the same iPad, in the same browser (usually Safari). A different iPad won't see your answers. If you clear Safari history, your answers are gone. Save a PDF of your results and commitment card — it's the safest way to keep them.

Tap "Start over" at the bottom of the survey to wipe everything and start fresh.

?
If something isn't working
Quick fixes
  • Buttons not working? Make sure you opened the link in Safari, not inside a Schoology or Classroom file viewer. The URL should look like username.github.io/….
  • Page looks weird? Pull down to refresh.
  • Lost your work? If auto-save didn't catch it, you'll need to rate again. Saving as PDF is your insurance.
One more thing
Finding out you don't care about something is just as valuable as finding out you do. The point isn't to lock you into a passion — it's to help you notice what you're actually drawn to right now, and to grow as a learner along the way. Have fun.
